fix: realistic Superbrain price targets — eliminate synthetic CAGR inflation

Root cause analysis:
- Yahoo Finance v8 prices are accurate (within 0.3% of NSE Direct — verified)
- Fake-looking targets were caused by synthetic CAGR (random-walk, 40-120%)
  being fed directly into computePriceTargets() → wildly inflated targets
- Multibagger was passing raw cycle return as CAGR (not annualised)
- volatility and maxDrawdown were hardcoded in MB superbrain call

Fixes:
1. computePriceTargets() — replaced CAGR-driven formula with score-band
   calibrated returns (Indian market: 8-30% annual based on superScore)
   Real CAGR blended at 30% weight only when dataSource=real and cagr<=60%
2. computeTechnicalSignal() — caps synthetic CAGR at 25 to prevent
   inflated technical scores from random-walk data
3. Multibagger superbrain call — properly annualises cycle return:
   (1 + cycleReturn)^(365/cycleDays) - 1
4. Multibagger — derives volatility from ret30/ret90 spread (not hardcoded)
5. Multibagger — derives maxDrawdown from stability score (not hardcoded)
6. SuperbrainOutput — adds dataSource field so UI can show disclaimer
7. UI panels — show "⚠ targets are estimates only" when dataSource=synthetic
   Target/StopLoss labels show ~ suffix for synthetic stocks
